Location And History:

The year 1921 witnessed the announcement of Prince Abdullah bin Al Hussein, may his soul rest in peace, the establishment of the Emirate of Jordan, which declared its independence on May 25, 1946 under the name "The Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan", and His Majesty the founding King Abdullah (I) bin Al Hussein became its constitutional monarch. The Hashemite Kingdom Jordan in the Middle East is situated east of Palestine (West Bank) and the Jordan River. It is located at the crossroads of the Asian, African, and European continents. Jordan shares international boundaries with Iraq, Palestine (West Bank), Saudi Arabia, and Syria, as well as sea borders with Egypt. The virtually landlocked country has a tiny outlet in the south that borders the Gulf of Aqaba (Red Sea). The nation has a shoreline on the Dead Sea, which is the lowest height on land on Earth at 430.5 meters below sea level.The Arabian Desert covers a large portion of Jordan. The nation has an area of 89,342 km2, making it somewhat smaller than Portugal or the state of Indiana in the United States.

Population:

Jordan is home to 9.9 million people (in 2017, including refugees of the Syrian Civil War). Amman (pop. 1.5 million) is the capital and largest city; around 4 million people reside in Greater Amman Municipality, accounting for nearly half of the country's population. The official language is Arabic, with English as a second language. Jordan has a largely Muslim population; over 92 percent of Jordanians practice Sunni Islam, the country's primary religion. Jordan's southern cities have the largest percentage of Muslim residents. Jordan has some of the world's earliest Christian communities, going back to the first century after Jesus' crucifixion, in the year AD. Christians now make up roughly 4% of the population, down from 20% in 1930, while their absolute number has increased. This is owing to high Muslim immigration rates into Jordan, greater Christian emigration rates to the West, and higher Muslim birth rates. According to a 2014 Orthodox Church estimate, Jordanian Christians number roughly 250,000 and are completely Arabic-speaking. Christians are extremely well incorporated into Jordanian society and have a great deal of freedom.

 

 

Climate:

Jordan's climate varies widely. In general, the further inland from the Mediterranean you go, the higher the temperature disparities and the less rainfall. The average elevation in the country is 812 m (2,664 ft) (SL). The highlands above the Jordan Valley, the mountains of the Dead Sea and Wadi Araba, and as far south as Ras Al-Naqab have a Mediterranean climate, but the country's eastern and northeastern regions are dry desert. Although the desert regions of the kingdom can reach high temperatures, the heat is normally reduced by low humidity and a daily wind, with chilly evenings.
