Amman Festival of Contemporary Dance Festival:

Jordan aims to introduce the contemporary aesthetics of dance techniques, with the first session held in Amman April 2009, hosted by the National Center for Culture and Arts King Hussein Foundation, and with the support of the Greater Amman Municipality, the Dutch Embassy, and the Embassy of Korea in Amman, within a well-defined and systematic plan with the aim of introducing the Jordanian public to the techniques and aesthetics of contemporary dance and providing the opportunity for Jordanian artists to attend workshops in contemporary dance and benefit from the multiple experiences of the participating teams.

 

 

The Jerash Festival for Culture and Arts

The Jerash Festival for Culture and Arts is an annually-held event held in Jerash, Jordan. It is part of the Jordan Festival, which aims to enrich cultural activities in Jordan. Founded in 1981 by Queen Noor, it features several shows performed by Jordanian, Arab and foreign artists.

In 2015, the festival hosted 40 different Jordanian artists in Jerash, along with popular Arab singers such as Najwa Karam, Maya Diab and Wael Kfouri. The festival is hailed as one of the largest cultural activities in the region with millions of people going to see it. The 31st Jerash Festival for Culture and Arts in 2016 attracted over 100,000 visitors and the attendance rates were greater than that of the year prior.
